<p><p>The Large Aperture Scintillometer market includes two main types of lenses: Plano-convex Lens and Fresnel Lens. </p><p>Plano-convex Lens is a simple lens with one flat surface and one convex surface, which is commonly used in scintillometers for its ease of manufacturing and cost-effectiveness. </p><p>Fresnel Lens, on the other hand, is a thin, lightweight lens composed of a series of concentric grooves that reduce the thickness and weight of the lens while maintaining its optical properties. This type of lens is often used in high-performance scintillometers for its superior light-gathering ability.</p></p>
