<p><p>Iron oxide pigments are available in two primary types: natural and synthetic. Natural iron oxide pigments are sourced from natural deposits and are known for their diverse range of colors and earthy tones. Synthetic iron oxide pigments, on the other hand, are manufactured through chemical processes and offer a wider variety of colors and shades. Both types of iron oxide pigments are commonly used in various industries such as construction, paints and coatings, plastics, and ceramics for their durability, UV resistance, and color consistency.</p></p>
