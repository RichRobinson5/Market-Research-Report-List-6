<p><p>Injectable targeted therapy includes various types of treatments aimed at specific molecules or pathways in the body to treat diseases such as cancer. Monoclonal antibodies are immune system proteins that target specific antigens. Small molecules are compounds that can easily enter cells and interact with specific proteins. Immunotoxins are molecules that combine a targeting component with a toxic payload to selectively kill cancer cells. Other types of targeted therapies include antibody-drug conjugates and cytokine therapy, each with unique mechanisms of action.</p></p>
