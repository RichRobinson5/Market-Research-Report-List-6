<p><p>Ophthalmic diagnostic equipment includes various types of devices used for evaluating and diagnosing eye conditions. Fundus cameras are used for imaging the back of the eye, while ophthalmoscopes are used to examine the interior of the eye. Refractors are used for measuring and correcting vision, and optical coherence tomography (OCT) provides detailed cross-sectional images of the eye. Other equipment includes corneal topography systems, slit lamps for examining the eye's structures, tonometers for measuring eye pressure, perimeters for testing visual fields, and retinal ultrasound imaging systems for assessing the retina's health.</p></p>
