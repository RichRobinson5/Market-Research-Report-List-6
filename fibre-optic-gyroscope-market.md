<p><p>A Fibre-Optic Gyroscope (FOG) is an advanced sensor technology that utilizes the principles of light interference to measure angular velocity. It operates by sending light beams through optical fibers twisted in a coil, with variations in the beam's phase shift providing accurate rotational measurements. FOGs are increasingly used in various sectors, including aerospace, defense, maritime, and automotive, thanks to their high precision, reliability, and resistance to environmental factors.</p><p>The Fibre-Optic Gyroscope Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.20% during the forecast period. <p><p>The fibre-optic gyroscope market is segmented into single-axis and multi-axis types. Single-axis gyroscopes measure rotation around one axis, making them ideal for applications requiring basic orientation, such as consumer electronics and automotive systems. Multi-axis gyroscopes, on the other hand, measure rotation around multiple axes, providing comprehensive spatial awareness for advanced applications like aerospace, robotics, and navigational systems. Both types utilize the properties of light in optical fibers to achieve high precision and reliability in motion sensing.</p></p>
