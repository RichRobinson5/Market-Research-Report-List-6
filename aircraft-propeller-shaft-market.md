<p><p>Aircraft propeller shafts come in various types to suit different aircraft needs. Tapered shafts are designed to fit into tapered hubs for a secure connection. Splined shafts have grooves that allow them to slide into corresponding splined hubs for easy installation. Flanged shafts have flanges at the end to bolt onto a hub, providing a sturdy connection. Each type offers unique advantages depending on the specific requirements of the aircraft, including ease of installation, security, and stability during operation.</p></p>
